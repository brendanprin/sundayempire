# Dynasty Contract Football League Constitution

## Article I. League Identity and Purpose

This league is a **12-team, full-roster dynasty fantasy football league** built around long-term roster management, player contracts, salary-cap strategy, rookie development, and competitive balance. Unless otherwise stated herein, the Commissioner is responsible for administering league operations and interpreting these rules in good faith.

The intent of this Constitution is to provide a clear, durable framework for league play. Owners are expected to act competitively, honestly, and in a manner consistent with both the letter and spirit of the rules.

---

## Article II. League Structure

1. The league shall consist of **12 teams**.
2. Teams may be organized into **two divisions of six teams each**, as determined by the Commissioner.
3. Each franchise is permanent. If an owner leaves the league, the replacement owner assumes control of the existing franchise, including its roster, contracts, cap situation, draft picks, and penalties.
4. This is a **full dynasty league**, meaning any player may be retained from season to season so long as he remains under contract and the team remains in compliance with roster and salary rules.
5. Each franchise operates under an annual **salary cap of $245**.
6. The league’s **hard cap is $300**. Any team above $300 at the start of a scoring period shall forfeit that matchup.

---

## Article III. Rosters and Lineups

### Section 1. Roster Size

1. Each team shall maintain **17 roster spots**.
2. All 17 roster spots must be filled at the conclusion of the annual main draft.
3. After the draft, teams are not required to continue carrying 17 players, provided they remain otherwise compliant with league rules.
4. Roster size may temporarily expand during the rookie draft, but each franchise must cut back down to 17 players within **48 hours** of the draft’s completion.

### Section 2. Starting Lineup

Each team shall start **9 players** each week in the following format:

* 1 Quarterback
* 1 QB/Flex
* 2 Running Backs
* 3 Wide Receivers
* 1 Tight End
* 1 Flex
* 1 Defense/Special Teams

There are **no kickers** in this league.

### Section 3. Scoring Eligibility

Only statistics accumulated by players in a team’s active starting lineup for that week shall count toward the team’s total score.

---

## Article IV. Injured Reserve

1. Each team shall have **2 Injured Reserve (IR) slots**.
2. Only players officially designated on **NFL Injured Reserve** are eligible for placement on IR. Suspended players are not eligible.
3. While a player remains on IR, the owning team may elect to receive an **insurance payment** based on the number of weeks remaining in the fantasy season:

   * Before the Week 6 matchup: **50%** of the player’s salary
   * Before the Week 11 matchup: **25%** of the player’s salary
   * After Week 11: **no insurance payment** is available
4. If a team accepts insurance on a player:

   * the player must remain on IR for the rest of the season;
   * the player may not be activated, cut, or traded during that season; and
   * the player’s contract otherwise remains in effect unless he is later waived in accordance with league rules.
5. If an insured IR player is cut, any cap penalties shall be calculated using the player’s **full contract salary**, not the reduced insurance amount.
6. If a player on IR becomes healthy and the team has **not** accepted insurance, the owner must either:

   * immediately activate the player to the active roster, or
   * immediately elect the available insurance option.
7. All players must be removed from IR by **May 1** of each league year, either by being restored to the active roster or waived. Any player not activated by May 1 may be returned to the free-agent pool by the Commissioner.

---

## Article V. Scoring

The league uses **head-to-head points scoring**. Each weekly matchup is decided by the total points scored by each team’s starting lineup.

### Section 1. Offensive Scoring

**Passing**

* Passing TD: 4 points
* Every 25 passing yards: 1 point
* 2-point passing conversion: 2 points
* Interception thrown: -2 points

**Rushing**

* Rushing TD: 6 points
* Every 10 rushing yards: 1 point
* 2-point rushing conversion: 2 points

**Receiving**

* Receiving TD: 6 points
* Reception: 0.5 points
* Every 10 receiving yards: 1 point
* 2-point receiving conversion: 2 points

**Miscellaneous Offense**

* Kick return or punt return TD: 6 points
* Fumble recovered for TD: 6 points
* Fumble lost: -2 points

### Section 2. Team Defense / Special Teams

Defense/Special Teams scoring shall follow the host platform’s **standard D/ST scoring settings**, unless otherwise announced before the season.

### Section 3. Weekly Results

Each head-to-head matchup shall result in a **win, loss, or tie**.

---

## Article VI. Regular Season, Standings, and Playoffs

1. The regular season shall last **13 weeks**.
2. Each team shall play:

   * every other team once;
   * one additional matchup against the team nearest it in the prior year’s standings (e.g., 1 vs. 2, 3 vs. 4, etc.); and
   * one additional matchup assigned by the Commissioner to complete the schedule.
3. The **top 6 teams** by record qualify for the championship playoffs.
4. The **top 2 seeds** receive a first-round bye.
5. The remaining **6 teams** enter the consolation bracket, with seeds 7 and 8 receiving a first-round bye.
6. The playoffs shall be played during **Weeks 14-16**.
7. The winner of the championship game shall be declared **League Champion**.

### Section 2. Tiebreakers

For standings, playoff seeding, or consolation placement, ties shall be resolved in the following order:

1. Head-to-head record among the tied teams
2. Total points scored during the regular season
3. Any remaining unresolved tie shall be broken by the Commissioner using the host platform’s next applicable tiebreaker or, if necessary, random draw

---

## Article VII. Salary Cap and Budget Compliance

1. Each team operates under a **$245 annual salary cap**.
2. Teams may temporarily exceed the $245 cap only as a result of an **in-season trade**, provided they return to compliance as required by league rules.
3. Any team **at or above the $245 cap** may bid a maximum of **$0** on waiver claims.
4. By **May 1**, after all expiring contracts are processed and impending free agents are dropped, each team must be compliant with the $245 cap.
5. Any player cut solely to achieve cap compliance shall enter free agency.
6. The league’s **hard cap is $300**. If a team’s salary exceeds $300 at the start of a matchup, that team shall forfeit the matchup.

---

## Article VIII. Contracts

### Section 1. Contract Length and Minimums

1. Player contracts may range from **1 to 4 years**.
2. The minimum contract is **1 year at $1**.
3. Any player with a salary **below $10** may be offered a maximum contract length of **3 years**.
4. The number of years offered to players during the main draft or free-agent acquisition period is at the discretion of the bidding team, subject to these limits.

### Section 2. Rookie Contracts

1. All rookies shall automatically receive a **1-year contract** with a **2-year team option**.
2. After the player’s rookie season, the owning team may elect to exercise the 2-year option at the existing salary.
3. The 2-year option must be exercised **in full**; the two years may not be exercised separately.

### Section 3. Expiring Contracts

1. On **May 1** of each year, any player without at least one additional year remaining on his contract shall be released to free agency.
2. The sole exception is a player retained under the **Franchise Tag**.

---

## Article IX. Franchise Tag

1. Each team may use **one Franchise Tag per season**.
2. A Franchise Tag applies a **1-year contract**.
3. The tagged salary shall be the **higher** of:

   * the average salary of the top tier at the player’s position, or
   * **120%** of the player’s previous salary
4. The top tier at each position is defined as follows:

   * Quarterback: Top 6
   * Running Back: Top 6
   * Wide Receiver: Top 9
   * Tight End: Top 3
   * Defense/Special Teams: Top 3
5. The same player may not receive the Franchise Tag from the same team in **consecutive seasons**.
6. A Franchise Tagged player may not be traded until **after Week 1**.

---

## Article X. Release, Dead Cap, and Cap Penalties

1. If a player is cut before his contract expires, the team incurs a salary-cap penalty for the remaining years of the contract.
2. Dead-cap penalties shall be assessed as follows, with all values **rounded up to the nearest whole dollar**:

   * **100%** of the player’s salary counts against the cap for the current season
   * If **3 years** remain after the current season: **75%** of salary applies the following season
   * If **2 years** remain after the current season: **50%** of salary applies the following season
   * If **1 year** remains after the current season: **25%** of salary applies the following season
3. To have the 100% cap penalty count in the **current season**, a player must be cut no later than the week of the trade deadline (**Week 11**).
4. Players may still be cut after the trade deadline, but resulting penalties shall extend into the **next two seasons** as applicable.
5. If a player officially retires from the NFL, he may be cut with **no cap penalty**.

---

## Article XI. Main Draft / Veteran Free-Agent Acquisition

### Section 1. Player Eligibility

1. Only players eligible to play in the NFL may be selected.
2. In the league’s inaugural season, all NFL players are eligible.
3. In each subsequent season, the player pool shall include all players not currently under an active league contract, including:

   * undrafted rookies,
   * players with expired contracts,
   * players who were cut, and
   * any player not previously under contract.

### Section 2. Draft Format

1. The annual main draft shall function as an **open bidding contract draft**.
2. An editable spreadsheet or league document shall be made available to all teams, organized by position and/or platform ranking.
3. Teams submit a proposed **salary** and **contract length** for any eligible player.
4. A bid must be equal to or greater than the current highest-valued bid, as determined by the bid valuation formula in Section 4 below.
5. If the highest bid for a player stands uncontested for **5 minutes**, the bidding team wins that player.

### Section 3. End-of-Draft and Blind Auction Procedures

1. If a potential winning bid is made within the final **24 hours** of the main draft window, that player shall move to a **blind auction** conducted during the following 24 hours.
2. If the draft ends while a player is still being actively contested, that player shall also move to blind auction.
3. If a team has not completed its roster at the end of the bidding and blind-auction process, a straight fill-in draft shall be held using the remaining unsigned players.
4. Players awarded in this emergency fill-in draft shall receive the **minimum contract**.
5. Only teams that were the leading bidder on a player during the final 24 hours of the main draft are eligible to bid on that player in blind auction.
6. In blind auction, an eligible team may not submit a lower offer than the one it held at the end of the regular bidding period.
7. Ties shall be resolved by **random draw**.

### Section 4. Bid Valuation Formula

Because contract length affects player value, bids are compared using the following formula:

> A player values a contract as though he will earn **50% of the annual salary** for each contract year not explicitly offered.

Examples:

* 1 year at $10 = value of $25
* 2 years at $10 = value of $30
* 3 years at $10 = value of $35
* 4 years at $10 = value of $40

Accordingly:

* A 1-year, $10 offer (value $25)
* would lose to a 4-year, $7 offer (value $28)
* but would beat a 4-year, $6 offer (value $24)

The Commissioner’s bid sheet or formula is final for determining bid value.

---

## Article XII. Trades

1. Teams may trade players, draft picks, or combinations thereof from the conclusion of the main draft until the start of the first **Week 11** game.
2. No trades may be completed between the start of the first Week 11 game and the end of the NFL season.
3. Trading shall reopen at the conclusion of the fantasy season and remain open through the next main draft period.
4. In any trade, **each team must send at least one player**.
5. Rookie draft picks may be traded.
6. Upon trade, the receiving team assumes the player’s **entire salary and contract**. The player’s salary no longer counts against the cap of the team trading him away.
7. After any trade is processed, both teams must be compliant with roster and salary rules. Players may be dropped as part of the trade transaction to restore compliance.

---

## Article XIII. In-Season Free Agency and Waivers

1. In-season free agency shall use the host platform’s **blind bidding waiver system**.
2. There shall be **two waiver runs each week**:

   * one before the Thursday game, and
   * one on Saturday
3. Any team at or above the **$245 salary cap** may not bid more than **$0** on a free agent.
4. If the host platform cannot correctly enforce this restriction, affected owners are responsible for notifying the Commissioner so the transaction may be corrected.
5. Any player acquired through waivers shall be on contract **only through the end of the current season**, unless retained through a valid Franchise Tag.

---

## Article XIV. Rookie Draft

1. The rookie draft shall consist of **2 rounds**, plus an optional **bonus pick** determined by league tradition or Commissioner-announced contest, for a maximum of **25 total picks**.
2. The rookie draft is a **straight draft**.
3. The rookie draft order shall be based on the previous season’s standings and consolation results as follows:

   * 7th place
   * 8th place
   * 9th place
   * 10th place
   * 11th place
   * 12th place
   * 5th place
   * 6th place
   * 3rd place
   * 4th place
   * 2nd place
   * 1st place
4. The winner of the consolation playoffs receives the first rookie selection under the league’s established order.
5. Teams may choose to **pass** on rookie draft picks.

### Section 2. Rookie Salaries

Base rookie contract values shall be as follows:

* Picks 1-3: **$5**
* Remaining 1st-round picks: **$3**
* 2nd-round picks: **$1**

All rookies are subject to the rookie contract and option rules in Article VIII.

---

## Article XV. Forfeiture, Compliance, and Commissioner Authority

1. A team that is out of compliance with roster or budget limits may not accumulate statistics until the issue is corrected.
2. The Commissioner shall make reasonable efforts to notify teams of compliance issues in a timely manner.
3. If an owner fails to respond and remedy the issue in a timely fashion, the Commissioner may take necessary action to restore compliance.
4. Any emergency compliance action taken by the Commissioner is final.
5. Owners may submit and update a ranked emergency drop list for use in the event of unresolved compliance issues.
6. Any such list may include only players with salaries greater than **$1**.

---

## Article XVI. Restricted Players

1. The league may maintain a **Restricted Players List** consisting of players deemed ineligible for ownership under league policy.
2. Placement on or removal from the Restricted Players List shall be at the Commissioner’s discretion, subject to any league-approved amendment process.
3. Restricted players may not be drafted, rostered, claimed, or traded.

---

## Article XVII. Rule Interpretation and Amendments

1. The Commissioner shall interpret these rules in good faith and with the goal of preserving competitive integrity.
2. Where this Constitution is silent, the Commissioner may issue a ruling consistent with league precedent and the spirit of the league.
3. The Commissioner may publish offseason deadlines, draft procedures, technical platform instructions, and administrative clarifications, provided they do not conflict with this Constitution.
4. Any formal amendment to this Constitution should be circulated to the league in writing before taking effect.

---

## Article XVIII. Final Notes

This Constitution is intended to preserve the strategic depth, humor, and long-term team-building spirit of dynasty contract football while providing a clear framework for fair play. Participation in the league constitutes agreement to abide by this Constitution and any valid league directives issued under it.
